Paddle Experience

Paddle Experience is the first project I have undertaken. It is an application, both for pc and mobile, that works as a paddle court booking platform. Developed in Java, this application provides a user registration system, managing in a relevant way the availability of the courts.

It was developed in early 2020 during my second univeristy year. It allowed me to take a first step towards the software development field, beggining to learn about implementation, as well as the appropriate design features that compose a good UI.

Quarantine Party

After my first project, I wanted to contiinue practising on my own. From that desire, a new challenge arose.

Quarantine Party is a mobile app developed in C++ by mid-2020. It consists of a mobile game where players are asked different questions and challenges, applying a penalty to those who not succeed on them. There are three different levels, that go from easier challenges to more complicated ones, as well as bigger penalties as levels increase.

As the first project I entirely developed on my own, I learned to search the necessary information on the internet. Being computer science a career in constant evolution, it is essential to be able to research about the used tools, and those yet to come.

Gym Fit

Gym Fit is the result of the first software deveolpment group project I participated in. The app consists of a gym spot reservation platform, providing the user a way to schedule for a specific date and time, according to the gym's availability.

This project rised as a method of visualising the separation of an application in layers. As a consequence, teamworking was improved, learning to divide the pending work with my coworkers; as well as the knowledge of what implies the layer separation and its benefits.
